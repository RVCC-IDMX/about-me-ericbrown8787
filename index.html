Biography

Hello!

My name is Eric Brown. I'm 29 years old, and I graduated from Rutgers University with an English degree in 2014. Right now, I work as a low-voltage technician maintaining fire alarm and communication systems, and I'm going back to school at Raritan Valley Community College to study web development and programming. I like doing work where I get to build things that people can use, and I hope to be able to use the skills that I learn at RVCC to find a job in web development.

I had originally gone back to school to learn networking and IT support, but I started studying web development on my own time using The Odin Project and for this coming semester, I have decided to shift my focus to studying web development full time. Working on both The Odin Project and my classes at RVCC simultaneously has helped me learn to consider multiple approaches to solving problems, and I plan to continue with both going forward.

My Hobbies:

  • Building Computers

    I build and upgrade my daily driver desktop, and I like giving older computers that might otherwise end up as E-waste a second chance. I just finished Ship-Of-Theseus-ing my old computer, and I think my next project is going to be building and configuring a home server for network storage out of all of the spare parts.

  • Medieval Reenactment

    When I'm not working on computers or other electronics for business or pleasure, I like learning about what people did before we had any of that. My friends and I get together on weekends to sew, sing songs and hit each other with sticks(while wearing proper safety equipment, of course). You can check our parent organization out at Markland.org.

  • Reading

    I love science fiction and fantasy. Some of my recent favorite authors are Jeff Vandermeer, China Mieville and Tamsyn Muir.

  • Camping

    My partner and I are slowly working our way through camping in every New Jersey state park that allows camping. This year, we've been exploring the Pine Barrens.

  • Tabletop RPGs

    I play Dungeons and Dragons, as well as some quick and easy indie games when my regular group isn't feeling up to continuing the main campaign. I'm trying to work up the courage to attempt to run a game of Troika! right now.

  • PC Gaming

    ...when I have the time, at least. You can see some of what I've been up to lately on my Steam profile.

When they did feel my hands on them, they whinnied low as in joy, and licked at my hands and were quiet for a time. Many times through the night did I come to them, till it arrive to the cold hour when all nature is at lowest; and every time my coming was with quiet of them. In the cold hour the fire began to die, and I was about stepping forth to replenish it, for now the snow came in flying sweeps and with it a chill mist. Even in the dark there was a light of some kind, as there ever is over snow; and it seemed as though the snow-flurries and the wreaths of mist took shape as of women with trailing garments. All was in dead, grim silence only that the horses whinnied and cowered, as if in terror of the worst. I began to fear--horrible fears; but then came to me the sense of safety in that ring wherein I stood. I began, too, to think that my imaginings were of the night, and the gloom, and the unrest that I have gone through, and all the terrible anxiety. It was as though my memories of all Jonathan's horrid experience were befooling me; for the snow flakes and the mist began to wheel and circle round, till I could get as though a shadowy glimpse of those women that would have kissed him. And then the horses cowered lower and lower, and moaned in terror as men do in pain. Even the madness of fright was not to them, so that they could break away. I feared for my dear Madam Mina when these weird figures drew near and circled round. I looked at her, but she sat calm, and smiled at me; when I would have stepped to the fire to replenish it, she caught me and held me back, and whispered, like a voice that one hears in a dream, so low it was:--

No! No! Do not go without. Here you are safe!" I turned to her, and looking in her eyes, said:--

But you? It is for you that I fear!" whereat she laughed--a laugh, low and unreal, and said:--
